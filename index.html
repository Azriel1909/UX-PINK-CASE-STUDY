Project Description

PROJECT: GymTraini

ROLE: Interface, Interactions, Research

DURATION: Spring 2022 (Feb - April)

Project-Vision

We're building an app to help people find workouts that match their fitness level and view their progress from within the app. Before launch we need to find out if it is easy for users to identify their routines and training progress. We would like to understand what specific challenges our users may face in the process of finding exercises, adding them to their routine for the day and visualizing their progress, so we can help them solve those challenges. GymTraini is an attractive mobile application to take to all training sessions and meet your goals.

Kickoff

In the GymTraini design process, I opted for a design approach to short-term value delivery.Qualitative and quantitative research methods allowed for a simple and functional design. The methodology covers the following: interviews with users, questionnaires and usability testing sessions.The process was iterative and thanks to the feedback, I found agile ways to solve problems and deliver value to clients.

Ideation

I started by brainstorming, writing down all the possible ideas, until this point there was not a bad or good idea. I used affinity mapping to identify achievable GymTrani ideas. I consider that the brainstorming process allowed me to shape the complete picture of the product.

Research Questions

How long does it take for the user to find exercises that match their fitness level within the app?

What can we learn from the flow that the user follows to add the exercises to their training plan for the day?

Where users can get stuck within the app?

Do the daily routine recommendations help the user to make an easy decision?

Insight Identification

Users need a more intuitive way to access menu settings.

Users need better cues for what steps are required to create a routine.

Users need more information about the potential value of their workout level.

Users need better cues for what sections are required to edit.

Users need more information about the graphic visualization.

Pattern Identification

75% of users not everyone knows which workouts are right for their level of training

50% of users are not immediately clear on how to create a new routine
